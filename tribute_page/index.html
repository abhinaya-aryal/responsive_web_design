Nikola Tesla

The man with photographic memory

Here's a timeline of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Nikola Tesla was born on 10 July 1856. Tesla was the fourth of five childrean. His father was a priest of the Eastern Orthodox Church.
  • 1881 - Tesla moved to Budapest, Hungary in 1881 to work under Tivadar Puskas at a telegraph company, the Budapest Telephone Exchange.
  • 1882 - In 1882, Tivadar Puskas got Tesla another job in Paris with the Continental Company. Tesla began working in what was then a brand new industry, installing door incandescent lighting citywide in large scale electric power utility.
  • 1884 - Edison manager Charles Batchelor, who had been overseeing the Paris installation, was brought back to the United States to manage the Edison Machine Works, a manufacturing division situated in New York City, and asked that Tesla be brought to the United States as well.
  • 1885 - Soon after leaving the Edison company, Tesla was working on patenting an arc lighting system, possibly the same one he had developed at Edison.
  • 1887 - Tesla developed an induction motor that ran on alternating current(AC), a power system format that was rapidly expanding in Europe and the United States.
  • 1889 - Tesla Coil was developed and would be used to produce high-voltage, low-current, high frequency alternating-current electricity.
  • 1891 - On 30 July 1891, aged 35, Tesla became a naturalized citizen of the United States. In the same year, he patented his Tesla Coil.
  • 1895 - Edward Dean Adams, impressed with what he saw when he toured Tesla's lab, agreed to help found the Nikola Tesla Company, set up to fund, develop, and market a variety of previous Tesla patents and inventions as well as new ones.
  • 1898 - Tesla demonstrated a boat that used a coherer-based radio control which he dubbed telautomaton to the public during an electrical exhibition at Madison Square Garden.
  • 1906 - On his 50th birthday, he demonstrated a 200 horsepower 16,000 rpm bladeless turbine.
  • 1943 - At the age of 86, Tesla died alone in Room 3327 of the Hotel New Yorker. His body was found by maid Alice Monaghan when she entered Tesla's room, ignoring the "do not disturb" sign that Tesla had placed on his door two days earlier.
